That said, the **product positioning** you're describing is a much bigger idea.

### Cognitive Firewall

Sounds like:

> "Here's a technical system that detects ideological bias."

That's cool for an IITB hackathon. It's not something normal people wake up wanting.

### Nudgement

Now you're talking about behavior instead of security.

Instead of asking:

> "How biased am I?"

You're asking:

> "What direction are my habits nudging me?"

That's instantly easier to understand.

Imagine this.

```

```

No "bias score."

No political IQ.

No "truth percentage."

Just **where your information diet is drifting.**

That's actually interesting.

The word **nudge** is also psychologically loaded—in a good way.

People know what it means.

Algorithms don't "brainwash."

They **nudge.**

So the product becomes about:

> "See how the internet is nudging you."

That's a much cleaner story.

One thing I'd change though.

Don't make it only politics.

That's a ceiling.

Make **Nudgement** a general engine.

It could show nudges toward:

- political ideology
- consumerism
- doomscrolling
- productivity
- AI optimism vs skepticism
- finance risk
- health habits

Politics is just one lens.

I'd also avoid pretending the score is objective.

Instead of:

> "You're 73% left."

Do something like:

> "Based on the content you've consumed this week, your online exposure leans left."

That subtle wording matters because you're measuring **exposure**, not someone's beliefs.

The biggest opportunity, in my opinion, isn't the score.

It's the timeline.

Imagine opening the app and seeing:

```

Brand-wise:

- **Nudgement** ✅ memorable, sounds like "nudge" + "judgment"
- **Nudgemeter** ✅ nice feature name, not the product name
- **Cognitive Firewall** feels like infrastructure.
- **Nudgement** feels like a consumer product.

If I were pitching this today, I'd probably describe it in one sentence:

> **Nudgement is a personal algorithm audit that shows how your digital life is quietly nudging your worldview over time.**

That's a lot more compelling than "a cognitive firewall." The hackathon project sounds like the underlying technology; **Nudgement** sounds like something people might actually install.
